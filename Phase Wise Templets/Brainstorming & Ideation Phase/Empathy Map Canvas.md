__Empathy Map Canvas:__

An empathy map is a simple, easy\-to\-digest visual that captures knowledge about a user’s behaviours and attitudes\. 

It is a useful tool to helps teams better understand their users\.

Creating an effective solution requires understanding the true problem and the person who is experiencing it\. The exercise of creating the map helps participants consider things from the user’s perspective along with his or her goals and challenges\.

## ShopEZ Customer Empathy Map Canvas

### 1. Who are we empathizing with?
* **User:** Modern digital shoppers looking to purchase daily essentials, fashion, and electronics.
* **Situation:** Browsing storefronts on their computer or mobile device, trying to find deals and complete purchases without wasting time.

### 2. What do they need to DO?
* Quickly find products using category filters and price/rating sorting.
* Add desired items to a persistent cart that doesn't disappear on page refresh.
* Enter delivery details and pay securely using their preferred payment method.
* View their past order history to track transactions.

### 3. What do they SEE?
* Clean, vibrant layouts with high-quality product images, ratings, and clear discount badges.
* Interactive categories (Fashion, Electronics, Mobiles, Groceries, etc.) and search bars.
* A cart count indicator that updates instantly.
* Clear visual status messages (e.g., "In Stock" vs "Out of Stock").

### 4. What do they SAY?
* "I want to shop without dealing with slow pages or complex steps."
* "Is this site secure enough to handle my card and address?"
* "I hate when my shopping cart empties itself if I navigate away."
* "Can I filter out items that are too expensive or poorly rated?"

### 5. What do they DO?
* Browse multiple pages and compare prices/discounts.
* Click category links to narrow down products.
* Add items to the cart, adjust quantities, and proceed to the checkout form.
* Check their personal order dashboard after making a purchase.

### 6. What do they HEAR?
* Peers complaining about websites that crash during payment.
* Colleagues recommending fast, clean, single-page shopping experiences.
* Marketing ads promising "easy, secure online checkout."

### 7. What do they THINK & FEEL?
* **PAINS (Fears, Frustrations, Obstacles):**
  * Frustrated by slow page loads or clunky navigation.
  * Worried about password or payment details being exposed.
  * Annoyed by having to re-add cart items if the session expires.
  * Discouraged by hidden fees or unexpected stockouts at checkout.
* **GAINS (Wants, Needs, Success Criteria):**
  * Feels happy when the site responds instantly and checkout takes under a minute.
  * Appreciates transparent discount tags and ratings.
  * Enjoys the convenience of persistent cart storage.
  * Feels reassured by clear order confirmations.
